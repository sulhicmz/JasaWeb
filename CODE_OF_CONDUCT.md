# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism**
- **Focusing on what is best for the community**
- **Showing empathy towards other community members**
- **Helping others learn and grow**
- **Acknowledging and celebrating contributions**
- **Providing constructive feedback**
- **Being patient with newcomers**
- **Taking responsibility for mistakes and apologizing**

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual harassment, bullying, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory jokes, language, or actions based on personal characteristics
- **Personal Attacks**: Insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- **Disruption**: Deliberate disruption of discussions, meetings, or events
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing private information about others without permission
- **Spam**: Unsolicited advertising or promotional material
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or any other threatening behavior
- **Sabotage**: Deliberate attempts to undermine the project or community
- **Abuse of Power**: Using position or influence to inappropriately influence others

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it through one of these channels:

#### **Primary Contact**

- **Email**: conduct@jasaweb.com
- **Private Message**: Any maintainer on Discord or GitHub

#### **GitHub Issues**

For public issues related to conduct:

- Create an issue with the `conduct` label
- Mark as private if needed (contact maintainers directly)

#### **Discord**

- Send a direct message to any moderator or maintainer
- Use the `#moderation` channel (if available)

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

1. **Your contact information** (if you want a response)
2. **The people involved** (names, usernames)
3. **Description of what happened**
   4 **When and where it occurred** (date, time, location)
4. **Any evidence** (screenshots, logs, links)
5. **Witnesses** (if any)
6. **Desired outcome** (what you would like to happen)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible. We will:

- **Protect your privacy** and identity
- **Investigate thoroughly** and fairly
- **Take appropriate action** based on findings
- **Follow up with you** about the outcome

### Response Time

- **Urgent issues**: Within 24 hours
- **Standard reports**: Within 72 hours
- **Complex investigations**: Within 1 week (with updates)

## Enforcement Guidelines

### Investigation Process

1. **Initial Review**: Maintainers review the report within the response time
2. **Gathering Information**: Collect evidence and statements from all parties
3. **Evaluation**: Assess the situation against our Code of Conduct
4. **Decision**: Determine appropriate action based on severity
5. **Communication**: Inform all parties of the outcome
6. **Follow-up**: Monitor the situation and ensure resolution

### Consequences

Consequences for violating the Code of Conduct may include:

#### **First Offense**

- **Private warning** from maintainers
- **Request for public apology** (if appropriate)
- **Temporary suspension** from community spaces (1-7 days)
- **Required education** or training

#### **Second Offense**

- **Longer suspension** (1-4 weeks)
- **Loss of privileges** (commit access, moderator status)
- **Public acknowledgment** of the violation
- **Mandatory mediation** with affected parties

#### **Severe Offenses**

- **Permanent ban** from all community spaces
- **Removal from project** (if contributor)
- **Legal action** (if illegal activities)
- **Public statement** about the incident

#### **Examples of Severe Offenses**

- Threats of violence or harm
- Hate speech or discrimination
- Harassment or bullying
- Doxxing or privacy violations
- Illegal activities

### Appeal Process

If you believe a consequence is unfair:

1. **Submit appeal** to conduct@jasaweb.com within 7 days
2. **Include reasoning** and any additional evidence
3. **Review by different maintainers** not involved in original decision
4. **Final decision** within 14 days of appeal

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- **GitHub repositories** (issues, PRs, discussions)
- **Discord server** and other chat platforms
- **Community events** (meetings, conferences, workshops)
- **Email communications** related to the project
- **Social media** interactions in official project spaces
- **Private communications** when related to project activities

### Project vs. Personal Spaces

This Code of Conduct applies to project-related activities. Personal opinions expressed in personal spaces are not covered unless they:

- Reference the project or community members
- Impact the community environment
- Are made in a professional capacity related to the project

## Maintainer Responsibilities

### Our Commitment

As maintainers, we commit to:

- **Leading by example** in positive behavior
- **Enforcing this Code of Conduct** fairly and consistently
- **Responding promptly** to all reports
- **Protecting reporter confidentiality**
- **Providing clear explanations** for our decisions
- **Educating community members** about expectations
- **Regularly reviewing** and updating this policy

### Conflict Resolution

When maintainers disagree on enforcement:

1. **Discuss privately** among maintainers
2. **Seek external advice** if needed
3. **Majority vote** for final decision
4. **Document the process** and outcome
5. **Review and improve** our procedures

### Training

Maintainers will:

- **Receive training** on diversity, equity, and inclusion
- **Stay informed** about best practices for community management
- **Participate in** regular policy reviews
- **Share knowledge** with the community

## Community Guidelines

### Communication

#### **Be Respectful**

- Use inclusive language
- Respect different opinions and experiences
- Assume good intent
- Avoid making assumptions about others

#### **Be Constructive**

- Focus on ideas, not people
- Provide helpful feedback
- Acknowledge good work
- Suggest improvements

#### **Be Inclusive**

- Welcome newcomers
- Help others participate
- Consider different perspectives
- Create opportunities for all voices

### Collaboration

#### **Code Reviews**

- Be constructive and specific
- Explain the "why" behind suggestions
- Acknowledge effort and good work
- Help others learn and improve

#### **Discussions**

- Stay on topic
- Listen to others
- Share knowledge freely
- Ask questions respectfully

#### **Decision Making**

- Base decisions on merit
- Consider community impact
- Be transparent about process
- Welcome diverse perspectives

## Special Considerations

### New Contributors

We especially encourage:

- **Patience** with newcomers learning our processes
- **Mentorship** from experienced community members
- **Clear guidance** on expectations and norms
- **Celebration** of first contributions

### Diverse Perspectives

We value:

- **Different backgrounds** and experiences
- **Varied skill levels** and expertise
- **Multiple approaches** to problem-solving
- **Cultural differences** in communication

### Accessibility

We commit to:

- **Providing accessible** community spaces
- **Accommodating different needs** and abilities
- **Using inclusive communication** methods
- **Regularly reviewing** accessibility of our spaces

## Policy Evolution

### Review Process

This Code of Conduct will be:

- **Reviewed annually** by maintainers and community
- **Updated based on** community feedback and needs
- **Communicated clearly** when changes are made
- **Available in multiple formats** for accessibility

### Community Input

We welcome:

- **Suggestions for improvement**
- **Feedback on enforcement**

- **Reports of gaps or issues**

- **Publish updates** to this policy
- **Share enforcement statistics** (anonymized)
- **Explain changes** to the community
- **Document our processes**
